# Aomori-Ken Grants & Subsidies Reference

*For IT/data businesses establishing operations in the Misawa–Hachinohe corridor.*
*Verified March 2026. Links and amounts should be re-confirmed before application.*

---

## 1. Hachinohe City: IT企業立地促進事業補助金

**What**: Rent subsidy + employment incentive for IT businesses establishing offices in Hachinohe.

**Source**: [Hachinohe City Enterprise Recruitment Division](https://www.city.hachinohe.aomori.jp/soshikikarasagasu/sangyoroseika/kigyoyuchi/3846.html) (産業労政課 企業誘致推進グループ). Contact: 0178-43-9048.

### Rent Subsidy (賃料補助金)

**Amount**: 1/4 of office rent, but the cap depends on business type:
- Contact centers / back offices: up to ¥6,000,000/year
- **Software / digital content businesses: up to ¥2,000,000/year**

Period total cap: ¥18M (contact centers) / **¥6M (software businesses)**. Available for 3 years within a 5-year window starting 6 months after operations begin.

### Employment Incentive (雇用奨励金)

**Amount**: ¥300,000 per new local hire (must be a resident of the Hachinohe Area Alliance 八戸圏域連携中枢都市圏).

Cap: ¥60M (contact centers) / **¥12M (software businesses)**. Available for 3 years from start of operations.

### Critical Employment Requirements

**This is the hard part for a micro operation:**
- Rent subsidy requires **3+ employees** (for software businesses) who are local residents, employed 6+ months, at fiscal year-end
- Employment incentive requires **3+ employees** for software businesses (but pays from the 1st hire at ¥300K each)
- Employees can include dispatched workers (派遣職員), not just full-time hires

**Practical impact**: A solo founder does NOT qualify. You need at least 3 people employed locally. This could be 3 part-time workers doing data QA, or 2 part-timers plus 1 dispatched worker. The ¥300K/hire incentive partially offsets the cost of meeting the minimum.

### Eligibility

Must be a Hachinohe-recruited company (八戸市誘致企業) in one of: information processing (情報処理), internet services (インターネット付随サービス業), software development (ソフトウェア業), or digital content (デジタルコンテンツ業). Must operate in the city for 6+ months continuously.

A data product company generating synthetic datasets qualifies under information processing or software.

**Stackable**: Yes — city-level, can combine with prefectural subsidies.

## 2. Aomori Prefecture: IT・コンタクトセンター関連産業立地促進費補助金

**What**: Prefectural subsidy for IT and contact center businesses establishing operations in Aomori Prefecture.

**Amount**: Varies by program year. Covers portions of facility setup costs, equipment, and employment incentives.

**Eligibility**: IT-related businesses (IT関連産業) creating employment in the prefecture. Preference for businesses in designated promotion areas.

**Key detail**: This stacks with the Hachinohe city subsidy. A business could receive both the city rent subsidy and the prefectural establishment subsidy simultaneously.

**Application**: Through Aomori Prefecture's Commerce, Industry and Labor Department (商工労働部).

---

## 3. Hachinohe Trial Satellite Office Program (八戸市おためしサテライトオフィス)

**What**: A try-before-you-commit program where IT companies can trial working from Hachinohe.

**What's covered**:
- Travel costs (transportation subsidized)
- Free coworking space during the trial period
- Facilitated introductions to local business contacts, financial institutions, and government offices

**Duration**: Short-term trial (typically 1-2 weeks).

**Why it matters strategically**: This is the entry point for everything else. Participating creates:
1. A documented relationship with Hachinohe's enterprise recruitment office
2. Physical presence history in the municipality
3. Facilitated introductions to JA Oirase and other local institutions
4. Evidence of genuine interest in local operations (useful for visa applications)

**Application**: Contact Hachinohe City Enterprise Recruitment Division (企業立地推進課).

## 4. Misawa City: Enterprise Recruitment (企業誘致)

**What**: Misawa has general enterprise recruitment programs but no dedicated IT satellite office program comparable to Hachinohe's.

**Current status**: Misawa's programs are oriented toward defense-adjacent and manufacturing businesses given the US military base presence. The city does have enterprise recruitment staff and may offer case-by-case support.

**Recommendation**: Use Hachinohe as the primary point of contact for subsidies and satellite office programs. Misawa can be the residential base (especially given DoDEA spouse employment) while Hachinohe serves as the business operations hub — the cities are 25km apart.

## 5. National Programs Applicable in Aomori

### MAFF: AI and Data Contract Guidelines for Agriculture

**What**: Not a subsidy, but a regulatory framework. MAFF published guidelines (農業分野におけるAI・データに関する契約ガイドライン) establishing norms for how agricultural data products should be contracted, shared, and licensed. This provides legitimacy and a reference framework for selling synthetic agricultural data.

**Why it matters**: When approaching JA cooperatives or municipal agricultural offices, citing MAFF's own guidelines demonstrates that agricultural data products are a recognized and encouraged category — not a novel or suspicious business activity.

### J-Startup Regional / JETRO

**What**: JETRO provides support for foreign-affiliated companies establishing operations in Japan, including consultation, introductions, and sometimes subsidies.

**Applicability**: As a German UG subsidiary (GK), the business qualifies as a foreign-affiliated company. JETRO's Invest Japan division may provide additional facilitation, particularly for introductions to local government and financial institutions.

### IT導入補助金 (IT Introduction Subsidy)

**What**: National METI program subsidizing IT tool adoption by SMEs. Not directly applicable to the data company itself, but relevant as a sales channel — JA cooperatives and municipalities purchasing the synthetic dataset could potentially use IT導入補助金 to fund the purchase, reducing friction.

## 6. Subsidy Stacking Summary

A GK establishing data operations in the Hachinohe area could potentially access:

| Source | Subsidy | Max Annual Value (Software) | Duration | Employment Req |
|--------|---------|---------------------------|----------|----------------|
| Hachinohe City | IT rent subsidy | ¥2,000,000 | 3 years | 3+ local employees |
| Hachinohe City | Employment incentive | ¥300,000/hire | 3 years | 3+ local employees |
| Aomori Prefecture | IT establishment subsidy | Varies | One-time + ongoing | Check program |
| Hachinohe City | Trial Satellite Office | Travel + coworking | Trial period | None |
| National (JETRO) | Foreign business support | Consultation | Ongoing | None |

**Reality check on the rent subsidy**: For a software business, the cap is ¥2M/year (not ¥6M), totaling ¥6M over 3 years. And it requires 3+ local employees, which means the subsidy partially goes toward meeting its own employment requirement. The employment incentive (¥300K × 3 minimum hires = ¥900K) helps offset that cost. Net benefit after hiring 3 part-time local workers: still positive, but not as dramatic as the headline ¥6M/year figure suggests.

The Trial Satellite Office program and the relationship-building it enables may actually be more valuable than the financial subsidies themselves.

## 7. Application Sequence

The recommended order to maximize relationship-building and evidence accumulation:

1. **Trial Satellite Office** — Zero commitment, subsidized, creates relationships
2. **GK formation** (if not already done) — Requires separate process (see GK-FORMATION-CHECKLIST.md)
3. **JA Oirase introduction** — Via Hachinohe enterprise recruitment office relationship from step 1
4. **Hachinohe IT rent subsidy application** — With GK registered and office lease signed
5. **Aomori Prefecture IT subsidy application** — Stack on top of city subsidy
6. **JETRO consultation** — For additional support and introductions

## 9. Risks and Mitigations

**Risk**: Subsidies require local employment creation — specifically 3+ local employees for software businesses.

**Mitigation**: This is not trivial for a micro-SaaS. Options: (a) Hire 3 part-time workers for data quality review / Japanese-language QA at minimum wage (~¥900/hour × 20 hours/week × 3 people = ~¥280K/month). The rent subsidy (¥167K/month max) plus employment incentive (¥900K one-time) doesn't fully cover this ongoing cost. (b) Use dispatched workers (派遣職員) who count toward the headcount requirement — potentially cheaper if using a staffing agency for limited hours. (c) Accept that the rent subsidy may not be worth pursuing if the employment overhead exceeds the benefit. The Trial Satellite Office (no employment requirement) and the JA Oirase introduction (relationship-based, not subsidy-dependent) may be the real prizes.

**Risk**: Residence card requirement for JA Bank account.

**Mitigation**: The Trial Satellite Office and subsidy applications create a paper trail of genuine business activity. If the representative director obtains a Business Manager visa, the residence card requirement is met. Alternatively, appointing a Japan-resident representative director (e.g., a 司法書士 who provides this service) can bridge the gap.

**Risk**: Subsidy programs change annually.

**Mitigation**: All amounts and programs should be re-verified before application. Hachinohe's enterprise recruitment office is the single best point of contact for current program status.

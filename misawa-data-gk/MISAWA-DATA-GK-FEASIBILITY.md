# Misawa Data GK — Feasibility Assessment

> **Purpose:** Strategic planning document for establishing a Japanese GK as a data
> factory subsidiary of ML Upskill Agents UG, growing revenue remotely from Germany,
> and eventually qualifying for a Business Manager visa and permanent residency in Japan.
>
> **Date:** March 2026
> **Craziness Rating:** 4/10 — structurally sound, well-considered, execution-dependent
>
> **Key correction (March 2026):** The owner already accounted for the October 2025
> Business Manager Visa capital increase to ¥30M. The plan is to build up capital
> over time while living outside Japan and developing language skills. The ¥30M
> requirement was introduced to stop the "buy an AirBnB and call it a business"
> exploit, not to block genuinely productive foreign-owned businesses. The HSP
> (Highly Skilled Professional) points system is a pathway to accelerated permanent
> residency, not a separate visa category — it works in conjunction with qualifying
> visa statuses.

## The Plan

1. Contract with a local nominee director; form Misawa Data GK
2. Ship a Beelink system; nominee sets up Tailscale access (pay for electricity)
3. Generate specialized Japanese long-text datasets (housing management, immigration
   forms) — hard to replicate without proprietary local LLMs
4. Sell on JDEX as Misawa Data GK; reinvest all revenue (no distributions)
5. Buy cheap property in Misawa / Rokunohe / northern Hachinohe — chosen for:
   proximity to shinkansen, proximity to airport, proximity to American installation
   (family transition), affordable real estate with space for resident + mini server room
6. Hire part-timer (SOFA-status or local) to babysit systems as cheap employee
7. Grow revenue, expand systems, promote employee to full-time
8. Build capital toward ¥30M Business Manager Visa threshold while simultaneously
   working toward N2 (which also earns HSP points for accelerated PR)

**Strategic philosophy:** Japanese business culture is relationship-heavy, but the
plan deliberately avoids dependence on relationship-building. Instead, the strategy
is to put irresistibly good, hard-to-make long-text data out there and let quality
drive discovery. A portal for custom data orders could reduce the need for in-person
relationship building. Document automation SaaS is a natural evolution once the
local LLM capabilities mature, not the starting point.

## What Works

### GK Formation (Steps 1-2)

- GK registration: ~¥70,000-80,000 (vs ¥200K+ for KK)
- No notarization of articles required
- Single non-resident foreign member can own 100% (since March 2015 reform)
- No legal requirement for representative member to reside in Japan
- **But:** Need a resident Executive Manager (業務執行社員) for practical reasons
  (banking, mail, landlord interactions)
- Nominee services: ¥75,000-150,000/month from established providers
- **Risk:** Nominee carries full Companies Act liability — choose carefully

### Misawa Economics (Step 5)

- Land: ¥1,000-8,000/sqm (absurdly cheap)
- Apartments: ¥35,000-50,000/month
- Detached property with server room space: genuinely affordable
- Population ~37,000; ~10,000 connected to Misawa Air Base
- Low cost of living, low cost of operations

### Dataset Strategy (Steps 3-4)

- Housing management and immigration form datasets are a STRONGER commercial
  proposition than fisheries/agriculture data
- Broader demand surface: municipal governments, proptech, immigration law firms,
  bureaucratic modernization
- Hard to replicate without proprietary LLM pipeline running locally
- JDEX listing from a Japanese GK eliminates foreign-vendor friction
- **Potential micro-SaaS upgrade:** Don't just sell CSVs — build document automation
  tools (housing document generation, visa form validation) served via Cloudflare
  Tunnel from Beelink infrastructure

## What's Complicated

### SOFA Employment (Step 6)

- **Yes, SOFA dependents CAN work for Japanese companies**
- Requires written approval from sponsor's service component commander
- Subject to Japanese income tax on Japan-source income
- **Critical weakness:** SOFA status tied to military member's assignment cycle
  (2-3 year rotations). Your "server babysitter" will PCS and disappear.
- Acceptable for a transitional role, not a long-term foundation
- Alternative: hire a local retiree or part-timer directly

### Remote Management Substance

- No law prohibits remote GK management from Germany
- Banking is harder without face-to-face (some banks still require it)
- Tax audit risk: if Japan questions whether real business activities occur at
  the registered address, a humming Beelink alone may not satisfy
- Mitigation: ensure the nominee/executive manager handles some genuine
  operational tasks (customer communication, invoicing)

### Banking

- Japanese banks are notoriously difficult for new companies, especially with
  non-resident owners
- Some banks require in-person account opening
- Nominee representative can help, but expect friction
- Consider: PayPay Bank, GMO Aozora, or SBI Sumishin as more startup-friendly options

## Business Manager Visa Context (October 2025 Reform)

### Requirements (effective October 16, 2025)
- Capital: **¥30,000,000** (~€185,000 at current rates) — up from ¥5M
- Employees: **at least 1 full-time** (Japanese national, PR, or equivalent status)
- Management experience: **3 years** or equivalent education
- Language: **approximately N2 level**
- Physical presence: must be in Japan with genuine involvement

### Why This Doesn't Kill the Plan

The capital increase targets people who were buying an AirBnB with ¥5M and calling
it a business. A genuinely productive data factory that builds revenue, employs
people, and accumulates capital over years is exactly what the new rules are designed
to reward. The ¥30M is the target, not an obstacle — the plan builds toward it while
the owner lives outside Japan developing language skills and PhD credentials.

### HSP Points as PR Accelerator

The Highly Skilled Professional points system accelerates permanent residency
(70 points → 3-year PR track; 80 points → 1-year PR track). It works in conjunction
with a qualifying visa status (such as Business Manager), not as a replacement.

| Category | Criteria | Points |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Academic background | PhD (Computer Science) | 30 |
| Professional experience | 3-5 years (varies) | 10-15 |
| Annual salary | Depends on GK revenue/salary | 0-40 |
| Age | Under 40 | 10-15 |
| Japanese language | N2 | 10 |
| Japanese language | N1 | 15 |
| Bonus: degree from ranked university | If applicable | 10 |
| Bonus: Japanese government project | If applicable | 10 |
| Bonus: business in designated field | IT/data science likely qualifies | 10 |

Finishing the PhD and passing N2 are the two highest-leverage actions for both
the Business Manager visa application and the HSP points that accelerate PR.

## Micro-SaaS Opportunity Flag

The housing management + immigration form datasets are not just data products.
They are the foundation for:

- **Immigration Form Wizard** — a document automation tool for immigration
  consultants (行政書士, gyōsei shoshi) that pre-fills and validates zairyū
  shikaku applications. Parallels Faculty Wizard architecture.
- **Tatemono Kanri Doc Generator** — a tool for property managers to generate
  compliant housing management documents. Recurring revenue from property
  management companies.

Both could run on Beelink infrastructure via Cloudflare Tunnel, exactly like
the simulation portal concept in the VilseckKI market insights doc. The data
factory builds the training data; the micro-SaaS monetizes it at higher margins
than selling raw datasets.

# SOFA Status & Personal Tax Implications

**Status:** US citizen, military spouse on SOFA status in Germany
**Entity Owned:** ML Upskill Agents UG (100% sole shareholder & Geschäftsführer)

---

## What SOFA Does and Does Not Protect

SOFA (Status of Forces Agreement) provides specific tax exemptions for income derived from US government employment and related activities. It does NOT provide blanket tax exemption for all income earned while stationed in Germany.

### SOFA Protects

- Income from US government employment (your spouse's DoDEA salary)
- Your own income from US government employment (if applicable)
- Personal VAT exemptions on personal purchases (VAT forms)
- Customs/duty exemptions on personal imports

### SOFA Does NOT Protect

- Income from privately-owned German businesses — this is the critical point
- Geschäftsführer salary drawn from your UG
- Profit distributions/dividends from your UG
- Any commercial use of SOFA tax-free purchasing privileges
- Business-related VAT (you cannot use personal VAT forms for UG purchases)

**Bottom line:** Your UG's existence and your role as its Geschäftsführer create a tax footprint that sits entirely outside SOFA protections.

## Your Three Tax Jurisdictions

### 1. Germany — Corporate (via the UG)

The UG itself pays Körperschaftsteuer + Solidaritätszuschlag + Gewerbesteuer on worldwide profits. This is unaffected by your SOFA status. The UG is a German legal person and is taxed as such. See `02-GERMAN-CORPORATE-TAX.md` for rates and calculations.

### 2. Germany — Personal (your Geschäftsführer salary)

Any salary you draw from the UG is subject to German personal income tax (Einkommensteuer). The UG must withhold Lohnsteuer (wage tax) monthly and remit it to the Finanzamt, just like any other employer.

German personal income tax is progressive (14%–45%). As a non-resident for German tax purposes (which SOFA status generally confers), you would be taxed at Steuerklasse I rates on your UG salary only — you would not be required to declare other worldwide income to Germany.

**Key question for your Steuerberater:** Whether you are classified as "beschränkt steuerpflichtig" (limited tax liability — non-resident) or "unbeschränkt steuerpflichtig" (unlimited tax liability — resident) for German personal income tax purposes. SOFA status normally keeps you in the "beschränkt" category, but owning and managing a German business may complicate this.

### 3. United States — Personal (IRS, worldwide income)

As a US citizen, you must file Form 1040 annually reporting ALL worldwide income, regardless of where you live. This includes:

- Any Geschäftsführer salary from the UG
- Any profit distributions from the UG
- Any other income

**Key US tax considerations:**

**Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE):** You may be able to exclude up to ~$130,000 (2026 limit, adjusted annually) of foreign earned income from US taxation. However, FEIE eligibility under SOFA status is uncertain. SOFA implies "intent to return to the US," which may conflict with the Bona Fide Residence Test. The Physical Presence Test (330+ days outside the US in 12 months) may be easier to meet. Discuss with a US expat tax advisor.

**Foreign Tax Credit (FTC):** If FEIE is unavailable or insufficient, you can claim credits for German taxes paid (both corporate taxes via the UG and personal income taxes on your salary) against your US tax liability. This avoids double taxation but requires careful coordination.

**FBAR (FinCEN 114):** If the aggregate value of your foreign financial accounts exceeds $10,000 at any point during the year, you must file FBAR. Your Wise Business account, any German bank accounts, and the UG's business accounts all count.

**FATCA (Form 8938):** If your foreign financial assets exceed $200,000 (end of year) or $300,000 (at any point during the year) for individuals living abroad, you must file Form 8938 with your tax return.

**Form 5471:** As a US person who owns more than 50% of a foreign corporation, you must file Form 5471 (Information Return of US Persons With Respect to Certain Foreign Corporations) annually. A UG is treated as a corporation for US tax purposes. This is a complex form — your US tax advisor should handle it.

**CFC Rules (Subpart F):** Your UG is a Controlled Foreign Corporation (CFC) for US tax purposes. Under GILTI (Global Intangible Low-Taxed Income) provisions, you may owe US tax on UG profits even if they are not distributed to you. The effective German tax rate (~28–30%) is likely high enough to largely offset GILTI liability, but this needs professional calculation.

## Practical Recommendations

### Geschäftsführer Salary Strategy

Your Steuerberater should help you determine the optimal salary level. Considerations:

**Option A: No salary initially (pre-revenue or minimal revenue)**
- The UG retains all cash for operations and the 25% GmbH reserve
- You report no German personal income from the UG
- US tax: you may still owe GILTI on UG profits, but at low profit levels this is minimal
- Risk: German tax authorities could impute a deemed salary ("verdeckte Gewinnausschüttung" risk is low when the UG genuinely cannot afford to pay)

**Option B: Minimum defensible salary (once revenue justifies it)**
- Set salary at a level that reflects actual work performed
- Common range for small UG Geschäftsführer: €1,000–3,000/month
- This creates a deductible expense for the UG (reducing corporate tax) but creates personal tax liability
- The net effect depends on the relative rates — your Steuerberater can model this

**Option C: Market-rate salary (once profitable)**
- Pay yourself a proper salary reflecting the market rate for your role
- Reduces UG corporate profits (and thus corporate tax + 25% retention requirement)
- Increases personal tax liability but may result in lower total tax burden depending on the math
- Must be "reasonable" — excessive salary will be reclassified as hidden profit distribution

### What Happens When SOFA Status Ends

When your spouse's DoDEA assignment ends and you leave SOFA status:

1. If you remain in Germany, you become a full German tax resident with unlimited tax liability
2. All worldwide income becomes subject to German taxation
3. You would need a German residence permit (separate from SOFA) — consult Ausländerbehörde
4. Your UG continues operating normally; the change is to your personal tax status
5. The US-Germany Double Taxation Treaty (DBA) becomes fully applicable

**Planning ahead:** If you anticipate leaving Germany when SOFA ends, the transition is cleaner — the UG continues as a German entity, you manage it remotely as a non-resident Geschäftsführer, and your personal tax situation simplifies to just US obligations (plus German withholding on salary).

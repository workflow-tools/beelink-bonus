## Executive Summary

VilseckKI Data Factory is a config-driven synthetic dataset generation pipeline
running on a Beelink GTR9 Pro (128GB unified memory, 70B-parameter LLM capability)
in Vilseck, Bavaria. The pipeline generates tabular + LLM-augmented datasets from
YAML configs, validates them with Evidently AI, and packages them with Gebru-style
datasheets for marketplace listing.

**The core hypothesis:** there is commercial demand for boutique, regionally
realistic, DSGVO-native synthetic datasets — particularly in German B2B data
and Japanese fisheries/agriculture data.

**The honest status of that hypothesis:** unproven. No transaction has occurred.
The regulatory drivers (EU AI Act, Japanese APPI) and marketplace infrastructure
(Datarade, JDEX) exist, but no evidence confirms that buyers are actively purchasing
the specific dataset types we plan to produce. The entire operation is a structured
experiment with bounded downside (the pipeline doubles as dissertation infrastructure).

## 1. The Product

### What We Generate

Config-driven synthetic datasets. Each dataset is defined by a single YAML file.
The pipeline:

1. **Tabular generation** — SDV (GaussianCopula default, CTGAN for production)
   creates statistically realistic numerical + categorical structure from seed
   distributions or real seed CSVs.
2. **LLM augmentation** — Ollama (llama3.1:70b locally) replaces text placeholders
   with contextually generated German or Japanese text. Async with semaphore
   concurrency control.
3. **Validation** — Evidently AI statistical quality reports + custom domain checks.
   PASS/FAIL gate before packaging.
4. **Packaging** — CSV + Parquet output, auto-generated Gebru-style datasheet,
   schema.json, preview sample (100-200 rows), ZIP archive.

### Current YAML Configs (validated against Pydantic schema)

| Config | Tables | Total Records | Language | Target Market |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------|---------------|
| `mittelstand-b2b.yaml` | 2 (companies, transactions) | 250K | de | German Mittelstand, EU AI Act compliance |
| `hachinohe-fisheries.yaml` | 4 (vessels, catch, ocean, auction) | 90.8K | ja | FRA Hachinohe, HIT, marine AI developers |
| `aomori-apple-orchards.yaml` | 4 (orchards, seasons, quality, auction) | 122K | ja | NARO, JA Aomori, agri-AI platforms |
| `test-tiny.yaml` | 1 | 100 | de | Pipeline verification only |

### What Makes These Different From Generic Synthetic Data

The competitive claim is **regional realism + domain depth + data sovereignty**:

- Distribution parameters sourced from named official statistics (Destatis, KfW,
  MAFF, FRA stock assessments) — not random guesses
- Japanese text generated by a 70B model running locally in Germany, not via
  US cloud APIs — chain-of-custody is verifiable
- Column schemas designed by domain research, not generic templates:
  - Fisheries: 12 species with Sanriku-realistic weights, CPUE, SST, Oyashio/Kuroshio
    current patterns, ikejime preservation methods, Hachinohe auction grades
  - Apple: 12 cultivars with Fuji-dominant weights, Chapman/Catlin phenological staging,
    Brix/firmness/starch index, JA cooperative grading, CA storage parameters
  - Mittelstand: 7 legal forms with Destatis-sourced weights, 12 industry sectors
    from KfW panel, Bavarian geographic overweighting, realistic lognormal revenue
    distributions

**Unresolved weakness:** A technically correct schema does not equal a paying buyer.
The schemas are defensible but the demand is not yet validated by any transaction.

## 2. Distribution Channels

### Datarade (Primary — German/EU Data)

- **What it is:** Berlin-based B2B data marketplace. 2,000+ providers. Commission-based.
- **How it works:** Vendor lists dataset with preview. Buyer discovers via search/category.
  Datarade handles invoicing and payment as intermediary.
- **Cost to list:** Free. Datarade takes commission on sales (exact % not publicly documented;
  reported as 20-30% range).
- **Tax implications:** You invoice Datarade (German B2B), not end customers. Standard
  German USt treatment. No need for tax infrastructure in 190 countries.
- **Relevant categories:** "Business Data," "Company Data," "Financial Data," "Compliance Data."
- **Fisheries/agriculture data on Datarade:** 258 marine data listings exist, but these are
  vessel tracking and oceanographic data — NO fisheries catch data products found.
  No apple/orchard agriculture datasets found.
- **Source:** https://datarade.ai/

### JDEX — Japan Data Exchange (Primary — Japanese Data)

- **What it is:** Japanese data marketplace operated by Kanematsu Corporation using
  Dawex technology. 260+ member organizations.
- **How it works:** One-time purchases AND subscription-based sales. File formats + API.
- **Foreign vendor support:** Platform designed to support international vendors.
  Registration free for basic membership; screening required before approval.
- **Identity verification:** Phone verification via voice call required.
- **Current status:** Operational. More mature than DATA-EX.
- **Fisheries/agriculture data on JDEX:** No evidence found of active listings in
  these categories. This is either an opportunity gap or a demand gap — unclear which.
- **Source:** https://j-dex.co.jp/en/

### DATA-EX (Monitor — Not Yet Viable)

- **What it is:** NOT a marketplace. It's a federated data infrastructure platform
  run by the Data Society Alliance (DSA), a non-profit consortium.
- **How it works:** Organizations build their own data spaces; DATA-EX provides
  interoperability infrastructure. Uses "data usage rights certificates."
- **Current status:** Still in development. Beta marketplace planned for FY2026.
  No public catalog visible. No published fee structure. No documentation on
  foreign company eligibility.
- **Verdict:** Not usable for post-and-list strategy. Monitor for when it matures.
- **Source:** https://data-society-alliance.org/data-ex/

### HuggingFace (Lead Generation — Both Markets)

- **What it is:** ML model and dataset hosting platform. Free tier sufficient for
  preview datasets.
- **How it works for us:** Host 100-200 row preview samples with full datasheets.
  Link to Datarade/JDEX for full versions. Builds academic credibility + citation
  count. Researchers discover via search, evaluate quality, then seek full dataset.
- **Not a direct revenue channel.** No one pays for data on HuggingFace. It's a funnel.
- **Source:** https://huggingface.co/

### AWS Data Exchange (Backup Option)

- **What it is:** Global cloud-based data marketplace. Japanese sellers eligible since 2020.
- **Consideration:** Requires hosting data on AWS infrastructure, which partially
  undermines the "no US cloud" positioning. But reaches a larger buyer base.
- **Source:** https://aws.amazon.com/data-exchange/

### Direct B2B (Future — Requires Relationships)

- **How it works:** Someone finds you via HuggingFace, vilseckki.de, or a personal
  introduction. You invoice them directly from the UG.
- **Tax treatment:** German buyer = 19% USt. EU B2B = reverse charge. Non-EU = no VAT.
- **When this matters:** Only after marketplace listings generate inbound interest
  or personal network produces a lead.

## 3. Market Demand Assessment — Germany

### What We Can Support

**EU AI Act is law.** Entered force August 2024. Article 10 requires high-risk AI
providers to document training data and test for bias. Obligations phasing through
2026-2027. This is not speculative.

**Germany has the most affected companies in the EU.** Largest economy, most
manufacturing AI, most HR-tech, most financial services AI. Annex III high-risk
categories hit German companies across:
- Recruitment/HR tools (CV screening, interview scoring)
- Credit scoring and financial risk assessment
- Medical device AI
- Industrial safety systems
- Biometric identification

**Datarade has established German B2B buyer traffic.** The marketplace exists,
German companies use it, and "Business Data" / "Compliance Data" are active categories.

**Emerging certification market:** TÜV AI certification, DIN standards bodies, and
Fraunhofer are all developing EU AI Act compliance tooling. These efforts may create
institutional demand for standardized test datasets.

**Bavarian AI Act Accelerator (LMU/TUM/UTN):** EUR 1.6M project through Dec 2026.
Bavarian SMEs participating in this programme need compliance test datasets.

### What We Cannot Support

- That anyone has specifically purchased a synthetic Mittelstand B2B dataset on Datarade.
- That EU AI Act compliance is currently driving synthetic data purchases (vs. companies
  still figuring out what they need).
- Specific price points for compliance-grade German test datasets. No published examples found.
- That demand is quantifiably larger than Japanese demand in dollar terms.

### Honest Assessment

The German market has the **shortest path to a first sale** because: the regulatory
forcing function exists (AI Act), the marketplace exists (Datarade), the buyer
population exists (German enterprises), and the friction is lowest (German UG
selling through German marketplace in EUR). This is different from "proven demand."
It is the best available first experiment, not a guaranteed revenue stream.

## 4. Market Demand Assessment — Japan

### Fisheries Data

**The privacy tension is real and well-documented:**
- Catch locations and CPUE are guarded as trade secrets by fishermen
- VMS (Vessel Monitoring System) data is legally confidential
- FRA needs realistic training data for stock assessment AI but cannot expose raw logs
- Published research documents fisherman resistance to data sharing: "concerns over
  potential unintended secondary uses of data"
- Source: https://www.facetsjournal.com/doi/10.1139/facets-2022-0153

**FRA Hachinohe is a real institutional target:**
- Fisheries Research and Education Agency operates a Fisheries Stock Assessment Center
  in Hachinohe
- Has a dedicated "Fisheries Data Sciences Division" conducting research on data
  collection and stock assessment methods
- Japan is actively trying to replace paper-based stock assessments (2-3 year data lag)
  with digital systems
- Source: https://www.fra.go.jp/english/shigen/center/fisheries_data_sciences_division.html

**Academic validation exists:**
- 2025 paper in Computers & Electronics in Agriculture: "Creating synthetic data sets
  for training of neural networks for automatic catch analysis in fisheries"
- Proves the approach works for fisheries ML applications
- Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0168169925002662

**Institutional targets in Hachinohe (from Gemini research):**
1. Hachinohe Institute of Technology (HIT) — Dept of System & Information Engineering.
   Regional Collaboration Research Center has formal cooperation with surrounding
   agricultural municipalities.
2. Hachinohe KOSEN — Dept of Industrial Systems Engineering. Active in applied ML,
   recent KAKENHI-funded publications on ML classification models.
3. Aomori Prefectural Industrial Technology Research Center (Hachinohe branch) —
   explicit mandate to help local SMEs modernize. Pipeline to sister Agricultural
   Research Institute.

**BUT — no commercial market exists:**
- Nearly ALL fisheries datasets globally are free (Global Fishing Watch, FAO FishStatJ,
  NOAA, Japanese e-Stat)
- No fisheries catch data products found on Datarade (258 marine listings are vessel
  tracking, not catch records)
- No evidence of fisheries data sold on JDEX or any Japanese platform
- No one has demonstrated willingness to pay for synthetic catch data specifically

### Apple / Orchard Data

**Research demand signals exist but are fragmented:**
- Orchard Robotics raised $22M (Feb 2025) to solve "farming's data problem" — but
  through sensor deployment, not dataset purchases
- AppleGrowthVision (CVPR 2025) created specifically because existing datasets
  "overlook different growth stages"
- NARO apple breeding programme uses DNA markers and relational databases, suggesting
  data infrastructure investment
- Japan smart agriculture market growing at 13.2% CAGR through 2034
- Source: https://www.thereportcubes.com/press-release/japan-smart-agriculture-market-report

**MAFF free data satisfies most aggregate demand:**
- e-Stat publishes prefecture-level production, yield, and variety statistics for free
- JA cooperatives control the detailed operational data and treat it as proprietary
- Source: https://www.maff.go.jp/e/data/stat/

**No commercial apple dataset marketplace exists in Japan.**

### Japan — Honest Assessment

The fisheries angle has stronger institutional demand signals than apple data, but
neither has a proven commercial market. The most likely path to a first Japanese
sale is **direct outreach to a researcher at FRA Hachinohe or HIT**, not a
marketplace listing. The JDEX listing is a low-cost experiment worth running
in parallel, but expectations should be set at "testing a hypothesis."

The owner has personal connections to Tohoku/Hokkaido agricultural cooperatives.
A single conversation with those contacts asking "would synthetic catch data or
agricultural transaction data be useful to you?" is worth more than all web research.

### The KAKENHI Grant Angle (Long-Term)

Academic researchers in Japan don't buy data out of pocket — they buy it using
JSPS KAKENHI (Grants-in-Aid for Scientific Research) funds. The play is not to
sell a finished dataset but to position VilseckKI as a vendor/subcontractor
that a researcher can write into their next grant proposal. This is a 12-18 month
relationship-building play, not a next-quarter revenue source. But it's the
most realistic path to institutional Japanese revenue.

## 7. Higher-Value Product Categories (Future)

### Synthetic Flawed Documents

Tabular data (rows of names, amounts, codes) is useful but commoditizable.
**Synthetic flawed documents** — realistic forms with identifiable compliance gaps —
are a fundamentally different and higher-value product category.

Examples:
- EHCPs with missing provisions, vague outcomes, undated reviews (tagged error taxonomy)
- Insurance claims with processing errors for claims-AI testing
- Medical forms with inconsistent entries for healthcare-AI validation
- German Mittelstand AGB contracts with deliberate compliance flaws

**Why this matters:** EU AI Act compliance testing needs not just "what does correct
data look like" but "what does subtly wrong data look like, and can your model
catch it?" A suite of 500 synthetic EHCPs with tagged error categories is something
a compliance tool vendor would pay for. A CSV of company names is not.

**Dissertation connection:** The owner's EHCP audit research (GRUND framework)
already requires building a taxonomy of common EHCP flaws. That taxonomy becomes
the error-injection config for a synthetic document generator. Research produces
the product spec for free.

**Pipeline architecture:** Different from tabular YAML configs. Requires multi-step
LLM pipeline: generate correct document → systematically inject realistic flaws via
error-category prompts → validate flaw tagging. Automatable on Beelink but needs
new pipeline code. Phase 2 product.

### Simulation Portal (Micro-SaaS Concept)

Instead of selling static CSVs, build a Streamlit web interface where a researcher
adjusts parameters (species mix, SST anomaly, fleet gear type) and generates
custom scenario data on demand. Beelink crunches locally, Cloudflare Tunnel
provides public access via sim.vilseckki.de.

**Parallels Faculty Wizard architecture** — single-purpose, domain-specific web tool.
Free 100-row preview generates leads; full 50K-row generation requires paid request.

**Status:** Concept only. Do not build until marketplace listings generate demand
signal. Estimated dev effort: 30-50 hours Streamlit + FastAPI + Cloudflare Tunnel
+ Japanese UI localization (requires native speaker).

**Documented in MicroSaaS-Ideas.docx** as potential future product.

## 8. Legal & Tax Considerations

### German UG Selling Data Products

- **VAT (USt):** Standard 19% for German B2B. Reverse charge for EU B2B. No VAT for
  non-EU B2B (Japan, US). Marketplace sales via Datarade: you invoice Datarade (German
  B2B), they handle end-customer billing.
- **Data product classification:** Likely "elektronisch erbrachte Dienstleistung"
  (electronically supplied service). Confirm with Steuerberater for B2C edge cases.
- **DSGVO:** Synthetic data generated from distribution parameters (not from real PII)
  is not personal data under GDPR. If generating from real seed data, the synthesis
  process itself may require a legal basis. BfDI guidance on this is evolving.
- **EU AI Act documentation:** Gebru-style datasheets auto-generated by the pipeline
  align with Annex IV documentation requirements. This is both a product feature
  and a compliance checkbox.

### Selling to Japan (APPI)

- **Cross-border data transfer:** Japan received EU adequacy decision in 2019. Synthetic
  data (not derived from real Japanese PII) likely exempt, but confirm.
- **Consumption tax:** Japan's 10% consumption tax applies to cross-border B2B digital
  services. Confirm treatment when selling via JDEX vs. direct invoice.
- **Germany-Japan Double Taxation Treaty:** Exists. Covers data licence royalties.
- **JDEX intermediary:** If selling via JDEX, Kanematsu Corporation likely handles
  tax intermediation similar to Datarade's role in Germany.

### Grant Timing (Critical)

**Verbot des vorzeitigen Maßnahmebeginns:** German/Bavarian grants cannot reimburse
purchases made before the official Zuwendungsbescheid arrives. If applying for
Digitalbonus Bayern Plus (up to EUR 30K) to cover new hardware, do NOT purchase
hardware before receiving the Bescheid. Portal opens 1st working day of each month.

## 10. Key Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| No one buys synthetic datasets on marketplaces | Medium-High | Revenue = zero | Pipeline serves dissertation; bounded downside |
| Free government data satisfies all demand | Medium | Undermines differentiation | Focus on LLM-augmented text + domain realism that free data lacks |
| Datarade commission eats margins on low-price listings | Medium | Thin margins | Direct B2B for larger engagements if marketplace proves demand |
| JDEX rejects foreign vendor or requires Japanese entity | Low-Medium | Blocks Japan channel | Fall back to HuggingFace + direct outreach |
| EU AI Act compliance demand is slower than expected | Medium | Delayed German demand | Regulatory timeline is published; obligations are phased, not optional |
| Someone with more resources enters the niche | Low | Competition | Domain depth (German Mittelstand, Sanriku fisheries) is harder to replicate than infrastructure |
| Beelink ROCm/GPU issues prevent 70B model from running | Low-Medium | Quality ceiling drops | Fall back to 7B Mistral for text augmentation; tabular generation is CPU-only |
